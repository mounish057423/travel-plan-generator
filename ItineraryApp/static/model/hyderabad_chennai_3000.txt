Okay, here's a detailed trip plan from Hyderabad to Chennai for 7 days, focusing on a mix of historical sightseeing, cultural experiences, and delicious food, all within a budget of $3000.  This plan assumes you are traveling as a couple.  I've included suggestions for hotels and flights, and resources for booking and further research.

**Assumptions:**

*   **Travelers:** 2 Adults
*   **Duration:** 7 Days / 6 Nights
*   **Budget:** $3000 (approximately INR 240,000 at today's exchange rate - can fluctuate)
*   **Travel Style:** Comfortable, mid-range accommodations, mix of private and public transportation.
*   **Currency:** INR (Indian Rupees) - costs given in INR and then converted to USD for clarity.
*   **Flights:** Economy class
*   **Hotels:** Mid-range, 3-4 star hotels with good reviews.

**Important Notes:**

*   **Booking:** Book flights and hotels in advance, especially if traveling during peak season (October-March). Use travel websites (listed below) to compare prices.
*   **Flexibility:** This is a suggested itinerary.  Feel free to adjust it based on your interests and preferences.
*   **Safety:** Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.  Keep valuables secure.  Drink bottled water.
*   **Bargaining:** Bargaining is common in local markets.
*   **Transportation:**  Consider using Uber/Ola for shorter distances within cities.  Pre-booked taxis are good for airport transfers.

**I. Pre-Trip Planning:**

1.  **Visa:** Ensure you have the necessary visa to enter India if you are not an Indian citizen.
2.  **Passport Validity:** Make sure your passport is valid for at least six months beyond your intended stay.
3.  **Travel Insurance:** Purchase comprehensive travel insurance covering medical emergencies, trip cancellations, and lost luggage.
4.  **Currency Exchange:** Exchange some currency to INR before your trip for immediate expenses.  You can also use ATMs in India.
5.  **SIM Card:** Consider purchasing an Indian SIM card upon arrival for local calls and data.
6.  **Apps:** Download useful apps:
    *   Uber/Ola (for ride-hailing)
    *   Google Translate (for language assistance)
    *   Zomato/Swiggy (for food delivery and restaurant discovery)
    *   IRCTC Rail Connect (for train bookings if you decide to take a train for part of the journey)
7.  **Clothing:** Pack light, comfortable clothing suitable for warm weather.  Include modest clothing for visiting religious sites.
8.  **Health:** Consult your doctor about necessary vaccinations and health precautions.

**III. Itinerary:**

**Day 1: Arrival in Hyderabad & Historical Immersion**

*   **Morning:**
    *   Arrive at Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD) in Hyderabad.
    *   Pre-booked taxi or Uber/Ola to your hotel.
    *   **Hotel Recommendation:**
        *   **Mid-Range:** The Park Hyderabad (Luxury), Radisson Blu Plaza Hotel Hyderabad Banjara Hills. (INR 6,000-10,000 per night or roughly $75-$125)
        *   **Budget-Friendly:** Hotel Minerva Grand, Taj Mahal Hotel (INR 3,000-5,000 or $37-$62)
    *   Check in and freshen up.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Charminar:** Visit the iconic Charminar.  Climb to the top for panoramic views (Entrance fee: INR 25 for Indians, INR 100 for foreigners).
    *   **Mecca Masjid:** Explore the historic Mecca Masjid, one of the largest mosques in India.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Laad Bazaar:** Wander through the bustling Laad Bazaar, known for its bangles, jewelry, and traditional Hyderabadi items. Bargain for the best prices.
    *   **Dinner:** Enjoy authentic Hyderabadi biryani at a local restaurant like Paradise Restaurant or Bawarchi. (INR 800-1500 for two or $10-$19)

**Day 2: Golconda Fort & Qutb Shahi Tombs**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Golconda Fort:** Visit Golconda Fort, a magnificent fortress with a rich history. Hire a guide to learn about its architectural marvels and legends.  (Entrance fee: INR 25 for Indians, INR 200 for foreigners). Consider the sound and light show in the evening (check timings and book in advance).
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Qutb Shahi Tombs:** Explore the Qutb Shahi Tombs, a complex of mausoleums showcasing Indo-Islamic architecture. (Entrance fee: INR 10 for Indians, INR 100 for foreigners).
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Hussain Sagar Lake:** Take a stroll along the Hussain Sagar Lake and enjoy the view of the Buddha statue. Consider a boat ride.
    *   **Dinner:** Indulge in Hyderabadi cuisine at a restaurant in Banjara Hills or Jubilee Hills. Try Haleem (seasonal dish).

**Day 3: Salar Jung Museum & Flight to Chennai**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Salar Jung Museum:** Visit the Salar Jung Museum, home to a vast collection of art, artifacts, and manuscripts from around the world. (Entrance fee: INR 50 for Indians, INR 150 for foreigners).  Allocate at least 3-4 hours.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   Lunch at a local restaurant near the museum.
    *   Check out of your hotel.
    *   Transfer to Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD).
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Flight to Chennai (MAA):** Book a flight from Hyderabad to Chennai (approx. 1 hour 15 min flight time).  SpiceJet, IndiGo, and Air India are common airlines.
    *   **Hotel Recommendation in Chennai:**
        *   **Mid-Range:** The Raintree Hotel, Anna Salai; Radisson Blu Hotel Chennai City Centre (INR 6,000-10,000 per night or roughly $75-$125)
        *   **Budget-Friendly:** Hotel Chennai Gateway, The Spring Hotel (INR 3,000-5,000 or $37-$62)
    *   Check into your hotel in Chennai.
    *   **Dinner:** Enjoy South Indian cuisine at a local restaurant. Try dosas, idlis, and uttapam.  (INR 600-1200 for two or $7.5-$15)

**Day 4:  Mahabalipuram - Ancient Rock-Cut Temples**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Day Trip to Mahabalipuram (Mamallapuram):** Hire a car or take a taxi to Mahabalipuram (approx. 1.5-2 hours drive from Chennai).  A day trip is highly recommended.
    *   **Shore Temple:** Explore the iconic Shore Temple, a UNESCO World Heritage site.
    *   **Five Rathas:** Visit the Pancha Rathas (Five Chariots), monolithic rock-cut temples dedicated to the Pandava brothers.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Arjuna's Penance:** Admire Arjuna's Penance, a massive bas-relief carving depicting scenes from the Mahabharata.
    *   **Lunch:** Have lunch at a restaurant in Mahabalipuram.  Seafood is a good option.
*   **Evening:**
    *   Return to Chennai.
    *   **Dinner:** Enjoy dinner at a restaurant in Chennai.

**Day 5: Chennai Cultural Exploration**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Fort St. George:** Visit Fort St. George, a historical fort that houses the Fort Museum and St. Mary's Church.
    *   **Government Museum:** Explore the Government Museum, one of the oldest museums in India, showcasing a diverse collection of art, archaeology, and numismatics.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Kapaleeshwarar Temple:** Visit the Kapaleeshwarar Temple, a vibrant and ancient Hindu temple dedicated to Lord Shiva. (Dress modestly).
    *   **Lunch:** Enjoy South Indian Thali at a restaurant near the temple.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Marina Beach:** Take a stroll along Marina Beach, one of the longest urban beaches in the world.
    *   **Dinner:** Enjoy dinner at a restaurant of your choice.

**Day 6:  Shopping & Relaxation**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Shopping:** Explore the shopping areas of Chennai:
        *   **T. Nagar:** Known for its silk sarees, jewelry, and textiles.
        *   **Pondy Bazaar:** Offers a wide range of clothing, footwear, and accessories at affordable prices.
        *   **Spencer Plaza:** A modern shopping mall with a variety of brands and restaurants.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Lunch:** Have lunch at a restaurant in the shopping area.
    *   **Relaxation:** Enjoy a relaxing spa treatment or Ayurvedic massage.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Cultural Performance:** Attend a classical dance or music performance (Bharatanatyam or Carnatic music). Check local listings for events.
    *   **Dinner:** Farewell dinner at a restaurant specializing in Chettinad cuisine (spicy and flavorful).

**Day 7: Departure**

*   **Morning:**
    *   Enjoy a leisurely breakfast at your hotel.
    *   Check out of your hotel.
    *   Transfer to Chennai International Airport (MAA) for your departure flight.

**VII. Important Considerations & Tips:**

*   **Bargaining:** Bargain respectfully in local markets and for auto-rickshaw fares.
*   **Tipping:** Tipping is customary in India (around 10% in restaurants).
*   **Water:** Drink bottled water to avoid stomach issues.
*   **Spicy Food:** Be mindful of the spice level in Indian food, especially Chettinad cuisine.
*   **Respect Local Customs:** Dress modestly when visiting religious sites. Remove your shoes before entering temples and mosques.
*   **Learn Basic Phrases:** Learning a few basic phrases in Hindi or Tamil will be helpful.
*   **Stay Connected:** Purchase a local SIM card or use international roaming to stay connected.
*   **Emergency Contacts:** Keep a list of emergency contacts, including your embassy or consulate.
*   **Photography:** Ask for permission before taking photos of people.
*   **Traffic:** Be prepared for heavy traffic in Chennai and Hyderabad.
