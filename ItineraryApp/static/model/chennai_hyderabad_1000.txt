*   **Morning (6:00 AM - 9:00 AM):**
    *   Travel to Chennai International Airport (MAA). Use Ola/Uber or a pre-booked taxi. Aim to arrive at least 2 hours before your flight.
    *   **Flight:** Book a morning flight from Chennai to Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD) in Hyderabad.  (e.g., IndiGo, SpiceJet).  *Book in advance for best prices!*
*   **Late Morning/Early Afternoon (9:00 AM - 1:00 PM):**
    *   Arrive at Hyderabad Airport. Take a pre-paid taxi or Ola/Uber to your hotel in the Charminar area or near a metro station for easy access.
    *   **Hotel Check-in:**
        *   **Budget Option:** Hotel Sitara Grand Ameerpet, Minerva Grand Kondapur (Check ratings, usually around $30-50/night)
        *   **Mid-Range Option:** The Park Hyderabad, Taj Deccan (Expect $75-$100/night)
*   **Afternoon (1:00 PM - 5:00 PM):**
    *   **Lunch:** Start with a delicious Hyderabadi Biryani at a local restaurant.  *Famous options:* Bawarchi Restaurant, Shadab Hotel (near Charminar).
    *   **Charminar:** Visit the iconic Charminar. Climb to the top for a panoramic view of the city.  *Entrance fee: Nominal.*
*   **Evening (5:00 PM - 9:00 PM):**
    *   **Laad Bazaar (Choodi Bazaar):**  Explore the vibrant Laad Bazaar, known for its bangles, jewelry, and traditional clothing. Bargain!
    *   **Mecca Masjid:** Visit the historic Mecca Masjid, one of the oldest and largest mosques in India.
    *   **Dinner:** Enjoy street food near Charminar or have dinner at a traditional Hyderabadi restaurant.  Try *Irani Chai* at a local cafe.

**Day 2: Golconda Fort & Qutb Shahi Tombs**

*   **Morning (9:00 AM - 1:00 PM):**
    *   **Golconda Fort:** Take an Ola/Uber or bus to Golconda Fort (about 11 km from Charminar). Explore the fort's impressive architecture, listen to the echoes at the Fateh Darwaza, and learn about its history.  *Hire a local guide for a more immersive experience.  Entrance fee applies.*
*   **Afternoon (1:00 PM - 5:00 PM):**
    *   **Lunch:** Have lunch near Golconda Fort. There are restaurants and food stalls available.
    *   **Qutb Shahi Tombs:** Visit the Qutb Shahi Tombs, a complex of tombs dedicated to the rulers of the Qutb Shahi dynasty. These tombs are a beautiful example of Indo-Islamic architecture. *Entrance fee applies.*
*   **Evening (5:00 PM - 9:00 PM):**
    *   **Hussain Sagar Lake:** Head to Hussain Sagar Lake, a large artificial lake.
    *   **Lumbini Park:** Relax at Lumbini Park, located on the banks of Hussain Sagar Lake. You can take a boat ride on the lake, including a ride to the giant Buddha statue on the island. *Boat ride tickets available.*
    *   **Dinner:**  Enjoy dinner at a restaurant near Hussain Sagar Lake. Options range from casual to fine dining.

**Day 3: Salar Jung Museum & Chowmahalla Palace**

*   **Morning (9:00 AM - 1:00 PM):**
    *   **Salar Jung Museum:** Visit the Salar Jung Museum, which houses a vast collection of art, artifacts, and manuscripts from around the world.  *Allow at least 3-4 hours to explore the museum.  Entrance fee applies.*
*   **Afternoon (1:00 PM - 5:00 PM):**
    *   **Lunch:** Have lunch near the Salar Jung Museum.
    *   **Chowmahalla Palace:** Visit the Chowmahalla Palace, the former seat of the Nizams of Hyderabad. Explore the palace's courtyards, halls, and museum.  *Entrance fee applies.*
*   **Evening (5:00 PM - 9:00 PM):**
    *   **Explore Old City:** Wander through the old city. Visit Moti Mahal and have some street food.
    *   **Dinner:** Paradise Restaurant (Multiple locations).

**Day 4: Ramoji Film City (Optional) or Shopping & Local Culture**

*   **Option 1: Ramoji Film City (Full Day):**
    *   **Morning (9:00 AM - 6:00 PM):** Dedicate the entire day to visiting Ramoji Film City, one of the largest film studios in the world. Explore the various sets, gardens, and attractions. *Book tickets online in advance. Transportation to Ramoji Film City will add to costs (Ola/Uber or a dedicated tour bus).*
*   **Option 2: Shopping & Local Culture (Flexible Schedule):**
    *   **Morning:** Shopping at Begum Bazaar or Sultan Bazaar for clothes, fabrics, and local goods.
    *   **Afternoon:** Visit a local market like Moazzam Jahi Market for fresh produce and spices. Enjoy a traditional Hyderabadi thali for lunch.
    *   **Evening:** Catch a Bollywood movie at a local cinema or attend a cultural performance (check local listings).

**Day 5: Departure**

*   **Morning:** Enjoy a leisurely breakfast at your hotel or a local cafe. Depending on your flight schedule, you might have time for some last-minute souvenir shopping.
*   **Travel to Airport:** Take an Ola/Uber or pre-booked taxi to Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD) for your flight back to Chennai.
*   **Flight:**  Take an afternoon or evening flight from Hyderabad to Chennai.

**IV.  Food Recommendations:**

*   **Hyderabadi Biryani:** Bawarchi, Shadab, Paradise (most famous, but can be crowded)
*   **Haleem (seasonal, Ramzan):** Pista House (very popular)
*   **Irani Chai & Osmania Biscuits:**  Nimrah Cafe & Bakery (near Charminar), Subhan Bakery
*   **Kebabs:**  Various street food stalls around Charminar and in the Old City.
*   **Double ka Meetha:**  Traditional Hyderabadi dessert (bread pudding).
*   **Qubani ka Meetha:**  Apricot-based dessert.

**V.  Important Notes & Tips:**

*   **Bargaining:**  Bargain when shopping in markets like Laad Bazaar and Begum Bazaar.
*   **Transportation:**  Use Ola/Uber for convenient transportation.  The Hyderabad Metro is a good option for traveling between certain points.
*   **Clothing:** Dress modestly when visiting religious sites (mosques, temples).  Comfortable shoes are essential for walking around historical sites.
*   **Water:** Stay hydrated, especially during the hotter months.  Drink bottled water or filtered water.
*   **Safety:** Be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against petty theft, especially in crowded areas.
*   **Language:** Hindi and English are widely spoken. Telugu is the local language.
*   **Currency:** The currency is the Indian Rupee (INR).  ATMs are widely available.
*   **Visa:** Ensure you have the correct visa for India.

**VI.  Customization:**

*   **Interests:** This itinerary can be customized based on your interests. If you're more interested in history, spend more time at the historical sites. If you're more interested in food, take a cooking class or go on a food tour.
*   **Budget:** You can adjust the budget by choosing more budget-friendly accommodation and transportation options.  Consider staying in hostels or guesthouses.
*   **Time of Year:** The best time to visit Hyderabad is during the winter months (October to March) when the weather is pleasant.

This is a comprehensive plan to get you started.  Remember to book flights and accommodation in advance, especially if traveling during peak season. Enjoy your trip to Hyderabad!
