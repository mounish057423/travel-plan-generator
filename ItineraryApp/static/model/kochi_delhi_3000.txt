Okay, here's a detailed trip plan for a Kochi to Delhi trip with a budget of $3000 (USD), focusing on historical sightseeing, cultural experiences, delicious food, and practical travel information.  This plan assumes a duration of approximately 7 days. I'll break down costs where possible, but remember these are estimates and can fluctuate depending on the time of year, specific choices, and availability.

**Assumptions:**

*   **Travelers:** 2 adults (budget is for two people)
*   **Currency Conversion:** I'm using an approximate conversion of $1 USD = ₹83 INR.  This can change, so check current rates.
*   **Travel Style:** Mid-range comfort with a focus on value and cultural immersion.

**I. Budget Breakdown (Approximate):**

*   **Flights (Roundtrip):** $800 - $1000 (Kochi to Delhi and back)
*   **Accommodation (6 Nights):** $600 - $900 (Avg. $100 - $150/night)
*   **Internal Transportation (Delhi):** $200 (Taxis, Metro, Auto-rickshaws)
*   **Food & Drink:** $600 (Avg. $50/day for two people)
*   **Activities & Entrance Fees:** $400 (Historical sites, cultural events)
*   **Miscellaneous/Buffer:** $200 (Souvenirs, unexpected expenses)

**II. Pre-Trip Planning (Crucial):**

*   **Flights:**
    *   **Travel Websites:** Use sites like Google Flights, Skyscanner, Kayak, and MakeMyTrip (India-specific) to compare prices.  Book in advance for better deals.
    *   **Airlines:** Consider IndiGo, SpiceJet, Air India, and Vistara (for domestic flights in India).
    *   **Example:** Look for a roundtrip flight from Kochi (COK) to Delhi (DEL).
*   **Accommodation:**
    *   **Hotel Booking Platforms:** Booking.com, Agoda, Expedia, Hotels.com, and TripAdvisor are good for comparing options.  Also, check India-specific sites like MakeMyTrip and GoIbibo.
    *   **Consider:** Hotels in Central Delhi (Connaught Place, Karol Bagh) or South Delhi (Hauz Khas, Greater Kailash) for good access to attractions.
    *   **Tip:** Read reviews carefully!
*   **Tourist Guides:**
    *   **Lonely Planet India, Rough Guide to India:**  Excellent for background information and practical tips.
    *   **Online Blogs & Forums:**  Search for Delhi travel blogs and forums on TripAdvisor or similar sites for up-to-date information and insider tips.
    *   **Local Guides:** Consider hiring a local guide for a day or two in Delhi for a more immersive experience. You can find them through tour operators or your hotel.
*   **Transportation Schedules:**
    *   **Delhi Metro:** The Delhi Metro is efficient and affordable.  Download the Delhi Metro Rail Corporation (DMRC) app for route information.
    *   **Buses:** DTC (Delhi Transport Corporation) operates a large bus network, but it can be crowded.
    *   **Taxis/Auto-rickshaws:** Uber and Ola (Indian ride-hailing app) are readily available.  Negotiate prices with auto-rickshaw drivers beforehand.
*   **Weather Forecasts:**
    *   Use reliable weather websites like AccuWeather, Weather.com, or the Indian Meteorological Department (IMD) website to check the forecast closer to your travel dates.  Delhi has extreme weather – hot summers (April-June) and cold winters (December-January).  October-November and February-March are generally pleasant.
*   **Visas:** Ensure you have the necessary visa for India well in advance.  Most nationalities can apply for an e-Visa online.

**III. Detailed Itinerary (7 Days):**

**Day 1: Arrival in Delhi & Old Delhi Immersion**

*   **Morning:**
    *   Fly from Kochi (COK) to Delhi (DEL).  (Allow ~3 hours flight time).
    *   Transfer to your hotel in Delhi (pre-booked). Consider these options:
        *   **Mid-Range:** Hotel The Royal Plaza (Connaught Place), Bloomrooms @ New Delhi Railway Station, The Park New Delhi (more upscale, but can sometimes have deals)
        *   **Budget:** Hotel Godwin Deluxe, Smyle Inn
    *   **Estimated Cost:** Flight - $400 - $500 (one way for 2), Taxi from Airport to Hotel - $10-15, Hotel - $100 - $150
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Old Delhi Exploration:** Take the Delhi Metro to Chandni Chowk.
    *   **Jama Masjid:** Visit the largest mosque in India. (Entrance fee: minimal, but photography fee applies).
    *   **Chandni Chowk Market:** Explore the bustling market, try street food (be cautious!), and shop for spices, textiles, and jewelry.
    *   **Raj Ghat:** Pay respects at the memorial to Mahatma Gandhi.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner in Old Delhi:** Karim's (famous for Mughlai cuisine) or try Parathe Wali Gali for stuffed Indian bread.
    *   **Estimated Cost:** Metro/Taxi - $5, Lunch - $15, Dinner - $20, Entrance fees - $5
    *   **Hotel :** Stay at pre booked hotel

**Day 2:  New Delhi & Historical Grandeur**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Humayun's Tomb:** A precursor to the Taj Mahal, this is a stunning example of Mughal architecture. (Entrance fee: ~₹600 for foreigners).
    *   **Lodi Garden:** Explore the beautiful Lodi Garden, which houses tombs and architectural works of the Lodi Dynasty.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **India Gate:** Visit the iconic India Gate, a war memorial.
    *   **Rajpath:** Stroll down Rajpath, the ceremonial boulevard leading to the Presidential Palace (Rashtrapati Bhavan).
    *   **Rashtrapati Bhavan Museum (Optional):** If time permits, visit the museum inside the Presidential Palace.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner:** Hauz Khas Village – trendy area with numerous restaurants offering various cuisines.  Try Social Offline or Elma's Bakery, Bar & Kitchen.
    *   **Estimated Cost:** Entrance fees - $15, Lunch - $20, Dinner - $30, Transportation - $10
    *   **Hotel :** Stay at pre booked hotel

**Day 3: Qutub Minar & Cultural Experiences**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Qutub Minar:** Explore the Qutub Minar complex, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, featuring the tallest minaret in India. (Entrance fee: ~₹600 for foreigners).
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Dilli Haat (INA):** Experience Indian handicrafts, food, and culture from various states. This is a great place to shop for souvenirs. (Entrance fee: nominal).
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Kingdom of Dreams (Gurgaon - optional):** If interested in a Bollywood-style theatrical performance, consider visiting Kingdom of Dreams (requires travel outside Delhi).
    *   **Dinner:**  Dilli Haat (try regional Indian cuisine) or explore restaurants in Connaught Place.
    *   **Estimated Cost:** Entrance fees - $10, Lunch - $20, Dinner - $25, Transportation - $15
    *   **Hotel :** Stay at pre booked hotel

**Day 4: Akshardham Temple & Relaxation**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Akshardham Temple:** A modern Hindu temple dedicated to Swaminarayan, known for its intricate carvings, exhibitions, and boat ride (optional). Note: No phones or cameras are allowed inside.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Relaxation/Shopping:**  Spend the afternoon relaxing at your hotel, exploring local markets, or doing some shopping.  Consider Janpath Market or Sarojini Nagar Market for affordable clothing and accessories (be prepared to bargain!).
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner:** Explore restaurants near your hotel.
    *   **Estimated Cost:** Akshardham (boat ride optional) - $10, Lunch - $20, Dinner - $25, Shopping - Variable, Transportation - $10
    *   **Hotel :** Stay at pre booked hotel

**Day 5: Red Fort & Museums**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Red Fort (Lal Qila):** Visit the historic Red Fort, the former residence of the Mughal emperors. (Entrance fee: ~₹600 for foreigners).  Allow ample time to explore.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **National Museum:**  Visit the National Museum of India to see a vast collection of artifacts and art from Indian history and culture.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner:** Daryaganj area - known for its bookstores and restaurants. Try Moti Mahal Delux Tandoori Trail (inventors of Butter Chicken).
    *   **Estimated Cost:** Entrance fees - $15, Lunch - $20, Dinner - $30, Transportation - $10
    *   **Hotel :** Stay at pre booked hotel

**Day 6: Crafts Museum & Garden of Five Senses**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **National Crafts Museum & Hastkala Academy:** A beautiful museum showcasing traditional Indian crafts.  Often has live demonstrations.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Garden of Five Senses:**  A unique garden designed to stimulate the five senses with its diverse flora, sculptures, and water features.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner:** Khan Market - upscale market with a variety of restaurants and cafes. Try SodaBottleOpenerWala (Parsi cuisine) or The Big Chill Cafe.
    *   **Estimated Cost:** Entrance fees - $10, Lunch - $25, Dinner - $35, Transportation - $15
    *   **Hotel :** Stay at pre booked hotel

**Day 7: Departure**

*   **Morning:**
    *   Enjoy a leisurely breakfast.  Do some last-minute souvenir shopping.
    *   Check out of your hotel.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   Transfer to Delhi Airport (DEL) for your flight back to Kochi (COK).
    *   **Estimated Cost:** Taxi to Airport - $10-15, Flight - $400 - $500 (one way for 2)

**IV. Food Recommendations:**

*   **Street Food (Use caution and choose reputable vendors):**
    *   **Old Delhi:** Parathe Wali Gali, Chole Bhature, Dahi Bhalla, Kebabs
    *   **Everywhere:** Samosas, Gol Gappe (Pani Puri)
*   **Restaurants:**
    *   **Moti Mahal Delux:** Butter Chicken
    *   **Karim's:** Mughlai cuisine
    *   **SodaBottleOpenerWala:** Parsi cuisine
    *   **Indian Accent:** (More expensive, but highly rated for modern Indian cuisine)
    *   **Regional Cuisine:** Explore restaurants specializing in Rajasthani, South Indian, or Bengali food.
*   **Drinks:**
    *   **Lassi:** A yogurt-based drink.
    *   **Chai (Tea):** Available everywhere.

**V. Important Tips:**

*   **Bargaining:** Bargain respectfully in markets and with auto-rickshaw drivers.
*   **Dress Code:** Dress modestly, especially when visiting religious sites. Cover shoulders and knees.
*   **Health:**
    *   Drink bottled water only.
    *   Be cautious with street food. Choose vendors that look clean and have a high turnover.
    *   Consider travel insurance.
*   **Safety:** Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas.
*   **Respect Local Customs:** Be mindful of local customs and traditions.
*   **Tipping:** Tipping is customary in India (around 10%).
*   **Learn Basic Hindi Phrases:** Knowing a few basic Hindi phrases will be helpful.  "Namaste" (hello), "Shukriya" (thank you), "Kitna hai?" (how much?).
*   **Stay Connected:** Purchase an Indian SIM card for local calls and data.  Airtel and Jio are popular providers.

**VI.  Flexibility:**

This is a suggested itinerary.  Feel free to adjust it based on your interests and preferences. You can spend more time at sites you find particularly interesting or skip others altogether.

**VII.  Booking Recommendations:**

*   **Flights:** Book flights at least 2-3 months in advance, especially if traveling during peak season.
*   **Accommodation:** Book your hotel at least a month in advance.

This detailed plan should give you a great starting point for your trip from Kochi to Delhi! Remember to do your own research, read reviews, and customize the plan to fit your personal preferences.  Have a fantastic trip!
