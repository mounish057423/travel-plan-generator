**II. Detailed Itinerary (5 Days/4 Nights)**

**Day 1: Arrival in Jaipur & Old City Exploration**

*   **Morning:**
    *   Arrive at Jaipur International Airport (JAI) or Railway Station.
    *   Take a pre-paid taxi or Ola/Uber to your hotel in the Pink City/Bani Park area (Rs 200-400). Alternatively, take a local bus if you're feeling adventurous and want to save money (check schedules in advance).
    *   Check into your hotel/guesthouse and leave your luggage.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Hawa Mahal (Palace of Winds):** Iconic landmark. View from the outside is often sufficient.  Consider paying the entrance fee if you want to go inside (Rs 50 for Indians, Rs 200 for foreigners).  Take photos!
    *   **City Palace:** Museum showcasing royal artifacts and architecture (Rs 300 for Indians, Rs 700 for foreigners).  Consider spending more time here if you're very interested in history.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Jantar Mantar:** Astronomical observatory (Rs 100 for Indians, Rs 200 for foreigners).  Interesting to see the ancient instruments.
    *   **Street Food:** Explore the Pink City's markets and sample local delicacies like *Pyaaz Kachori*, *Mirchi Bada*, and *Lassi*.  Budget Rs 200-300 for dinner.
    *   **Optional:** Light and Sound Show at Amber Fort (check timings and prices).

**Day 2: Amber Fort & Royal Grandeur**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Amber Fort (Amer Fort):** Take a local bus (Rs 20-30) or an auto-rickshaw (negotiate the price, around Rs 200-300) to Amber Fort, about 11 km from Jaipur.
    *   Explore Amber Fort (Rs 100 for Indians, Rs 500 for foreigners).  Consider hiring a guide (negotiate the price, around Rs 300-500) to learn about the fort's history and architecture.  Elephant rides are available, but consider the ethical implications before participating.
    *   **Maota Lake:**  Located at the base of Amber Fort. Great photo opportunity.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Jaigarh Fort:** Overlooks Amber Fort.  Take a taxi or auto-rickshaw (negotiate the price) to Jaigarh Fort. (Rs 100 for Indians, Rs 350 for foreigners)
    *   **Nahargarh Fort:** Offers panoramic views of Jaipur city.  Best visited during sunset. (Rs 50 for Indians, Rs 200 for foreigners).
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner at LMB (Laxmi Misthan Bhandar):** Famous restaurant in the Pink City known for its Rajasthani cuisine.  Budget Rs 400-500.
    *   **Optional:**  Attend a traditional Rajasthani cultural performance (check for local listings).

**Day 3:  Culture & Markets**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Albert Hall Museum:** State museum of Rajasthan, showcasing a collection of artifacts (Rs 150 for Indians, Rs 300 for foreigners).
    *   **Sisodia Rani Garden:** A beautiful garden showcasing Mughal and Rajput architecture (Rs 50 for Indians, Rs 200 for foreigners).
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Shopping:** Explore the vibrant markets of Jaipur:
        *   **Bapu Bazaar:** Textiles, handicrafts, jewelry.
        *   **Johari Bazaar:** Jewelry (especially precious stones).
        *   **Tripolia Bazaar:** Bangles and textiles.
        *   **Chandpole Bazaar:** Traditional crafts.
    *   **Bargain hard!**
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Chokhi Dhani (Optional - consider budget):** A Rajasthani village resort offering cultural performances, traditional food, and crafts.  Entrance fees vary depending on the package (Rs 800-1200).  It can be touristy, but it's a good way to experience Rajasthani culture in one place.
    *   **Alternative:**  Enjoy a local dinner at a restaurant in Bani Park (more modern and varied cuisine).

**Day 4:  Galtaji Temple & Relaxation**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Galtaji Temple (Monkey Temple):** A Hindu pilgrimage site with temples and natural springs.  Beware of monkeys!  (No entrance fee, but donations are appreciated.)
    *   **Sun Temple:** Close to Galtaji Temple. Offers good views.
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Relaxation/Free Time:**  Go for a walk in a park, visit a cafe, or simply relax at your hotel.
    *   **Optional:** Cooking class to learn how to make Rajasthani dishes.
*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner at Niros:** Another popular restaurant in Jaipur, known for its Indian and Continental cuisine.  Budget Rs 500-600.
    *   **Last-minute souvenir shopping.**

**Day 5: Departure**

*   **Morning:**
    *   Enjoy a final breakfast in Jaipur.
    *   Depending on your flight schedule, you may have time for some last-minute sightseeing or shopping.
    *   **Birla Mandir:** A modern temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu and Goddess Lakshmi (if time permits, near the airport).
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   Transfer to Jaipur International Airport (JAI) for your flight back to Chennai (MAA).

**IV. Important Considerations & Tips:**

*   **Weather:** Jaipur can be very hot, especially during the summer months (April-June). Pack light, breathable clothing, sunscreen, and a hat.
*   **Bargaining:** Bargaining is essential in the markets. Start low and be prepared to walk away.
*   **Safety:** Be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against petty theft. Avoid walking alone at night in poorly lit areas.
*   **Health:** Drink bottled water only. Avoid eating from street vendors that don't appear clean. Carry hand sanitizer.
*   **Dress Code:** Dress modestly when visiting temples and religious sites (cover shoulders and knees).
*   **Language:** Hindi and English are widely spoken.
*   **Transportation:** Ola and Uber are available, but auto-rickshaws are also a good option for shorter distances.  Negotiate the price before getting in.  Local buses are the cheapest option.
*   **Currency Exchange:** Exchange currency at the airport or at reputable money changers in the city.
*   **Sim Card:** Consider buying a local SIM card for data and calls.
