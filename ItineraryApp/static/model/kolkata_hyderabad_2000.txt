Okay, here's a detailed trip plan from Kolkata to Hyderabad, balancing historical sightseeing, cultural experiences, and delicious food within a budget of $2000 (approximately INR 166,000 as of Oct 26, 2023).  This itinerary assumes a trip length of 7 days/6 nights, but can be adjusted.

**Assumptions:**

*   **Travelers:**  2 people (budget split)
*   **Budget:** $2000 (for the *entire* trip: flights, accommodation, food, activities, transport, and miscellaneous).  We'll aim to stick close to this.
*   **Dates:** Consider the best time to visit Hyderabad is during the winter months (October to March) when the weather is pleasant.
*   **Currency Conversion:**  I'll provide approximate INR equivalents based on a rate of $1 = INR 83.
*   **Flexibility:**  This is a suggested itinerary; you can customize it based on your interests.
*   **Travel Style:** Comfortable but budget-conscious.

**I. Pre-Trip Planning & Booking (Crucial!)**

*   **Flights:**
    *   **Research:** Use websites like Skyscanner ([https://www.skyscanner.com/](https://www.skyscanner.com/)), Google Flights ([https://www.google.com/flights](https://www.google.com/flights)), MakeMyTrip ([https://www.makemytrip.com/](https://www.makemytrip.com/)), and EaseMyTrip ([https://www.easemytrip.com/](https://www.easemytrip.com/)) to compare flight prices.  Book in advance (at least 1-2 months) for better deals.
    *   **Airlines:** Consider IndiGo, SpiceJet, Air India Express, and Vistara for potentially cheaper options.
    *   **Estimated Cost (Round Trip for 2):** $300 - $400 (INR 25,000 - INR 33,200). Aim to book flights that fall within this range.
    *   **Example Flight:**
        *   **Outbound:** Kolkata (CCU) to Hyderabad (HYD)
        *   **Return:** Hyderabad (HYD) to Kolkata (CCU)
*   **Accommodation:**
    *   **Booking Platforms:** Use Booking.com ([https://www.booking.com/](https://www.booking.com/)), Agoda ([https://www.agoda.com/](https://www.agoda.com/)), MakeMyTrip, and Airbnb ([https://www.airbnb.com/](https://www.airbnb.com/)) to find hotels.  Read reviews carefully.
    *   **Neighborhoods:**  Consider staying in areas like Banjara Hills, Jubilee Hills (slightly more upscale), Gachibowli (IT hub, modern), or Secunderabad (historical, good transport links).  Begumpet is also a decent option.
    *   **Hotel Type:**  Look for 3-star hotels or well-reviewed guesthouses/boutique hotels.  Consider options with breakfast included to save on meal costs.
    *   **Estimated Cost (6 Nights for 2):** $400 - $600 (INR 33,200 - INR 49,800).  This translates to roughly $67 - $100/night (INR 5,531 - INR 8,300/night).
    *   **Example Hotels (Research Prices & Availability):**
        *   **Hotel Minerva Grand, Secunderabad:**  Good reviews, central location.
        *   **The Park Hyderabad:** More upscale, but might find deals.
        *   **Hotel Deccan Errum Manzil:** Decent reviews, budget-friendly options.
        *   **Airbnb:** Look for private rooms or entire apartments in your chosen area.  Read reviews carefully.
*   **Travel Insurance:** Essential for unexpected medical issues, cancellations, or lost luggage.  Cost: $50 - $100 (INR 4,150- INR 8,300) for two people.

**II. Detailed Itinerary (7 Days / 6 Nights)**

**Day 1: Arrival in Hyderabad & Old City Exploration**

*   Morning: Arrive at Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD). Take a pre-paid taxi or Ola/Uber to your hotel. (Taxi Cost: INR 500 - INR 800).
*   Afternoon: Check into your hotel and freshen up.
*   Afternoon: **Charminar:**  Visit the iconic Charminar.  Climb to the top for panoramic views (if open).  (Entry Fee: INR 25 for Indians, INR 100 for foreigners).
*   Late Afternoon: **Laad Bazaar:**  Explore the bustling Laad Bazaar, famous for bangles, jewelry, and traditional Hyderabadi items. Bargain!
*   Evening: **Mecca Masjid:** Visit the grand Mecca Masjid, one of the oldest mosques in Hyderabad.
*   Dinner: Enjoy authentic Hyderabadi biryani at **Hotel Shadab** near Charminar (Budget: INR 600- INR 1000 for two).
*   Transport: Auto-rickshaws and taxis are readily available. Consider using Ola/Uber for convenience.  (Daily Transport Budget: INR 400 - INR 600).

**Day 2: Golconda Fort & Qutb Shahi Tombs**

*   Morning: **Golconda Fort:**  Explore the magnificent Golconda Fort. Hire a guide (optional, ~INR 500 - INR 800) to learn about its history and architecture.  Attend the evening sound and light show (check timings and book tickets in advance). (Entry Fee: INR 25 for Indians, INR 200 for foreigners).
*   Lunch:  Have lunch at a restaurant near Golconda Fort.  Try local Andhra cuisine. (Budget: INR 500- INR 800 for two).
*   Afternoon: **Qutb Shahi Tombs:** Visit the Qutb Shahi Tombs, a complex of impressive tombs of the Qutb Shahi dynasty. (Entry Fee: INR 20 for Indians, INR 100 for foreigners).
*   Evening: **Hussain Sagar Lake:** Enjoy a boat ride on Hussain Sagar Lake and admire the Buddha statue.
*   Dinner: Dine at **Paradise Restaurant** for their famous biryani (Secunderabad branch). (Budget: INR 800 - INR 1200 for two).

**Day 3: Salar Jung Museum & Chowmahalla Palace**

*   Morning: **Salar Jung Museum:** Visit the Salar Jung Museum, home to a vast collection of art, artifacts, and manuscripts from around the world. (Entry Fee: INR 50 for Indians, INR 150 for foreigners).
*   Lunch: Have lunch at a restaurant near the museum. (Budget: INR 500- INR 800 for two).
*   Afternoon: **Chowmahalla Palace:** Explore the Chowmahalla Palace, the former residence of the Nizams of Hyderabad.  (Entry Fee: INR 80 for Indians, INR 200 for foreigners).
*   Evening: **Lumbini Park:** Relax at Lumbini Park, located near Hussain Sagar Lake.  Consider the laser show.
*   Dinner: Explore the street food options near Lumbini Park or have dinner at a restaurant in the Tank Bund area. (Budget: INR 600- INR 1000 for two).

**Day 4: Ramoji Film City**

*   Full Day: Dedicate the day to Ramoji Film City, one of the largest film studios in the world.  Enjoy the various attractions, shows, and gardens. (Entry Fee: Packages range from INR 1250 to INR 3500 per person; choose a package that suits your budget and interests).
*   Lunch: Included in some Ramoji Film City packages.  Otherwise, food options are available within the complex.
*   Dinner: Included in some Ramoji Film City packages.

**Day 5: Birla Mandir & Shopping**

*   Morning: **Birla Mandir:**  Visit the beautiful Birla Mandir, a white marble temple dedicated to Lord Venkateswara. Enjoy panoramic city views. (Free entry).
*   Lunch: Have lunch at a restaurant near Birla Mandir. (Budget: INR 500- INR 800 for two).
*   Afternoon: **Shopping:**
    *   **Koti:** For budget-friendly clothing, accessories, and electronics.
    *   **Abids:**  A mix of branded stores and street vendors.
    *   **GVK One Mall/City Centre Mall:**  For a modern shopping experience.
*   Evening: Relax and enjoy dinner at a restaurant of your choice.  (Budget: INR 800- INR 1200 for two).

**Day 6: Sudha Cars Museum & Culinary Delights**

*   Morning: **Sudha Cars Museum:** Visit this unique museum showcasing wacky and creative handmade cars. (Entry Fee: INR 50).
*   Lunch: Local Lunch (Budget: INR 500- INR 800 for two).
*   Afternoon:
    *   **Culinary Experience:** Take a cooking class focused on Hyderabadi cuisine. Search for local cooking classes on platforms like Airbnb Experiences or Cookly. (Cost: INR 1500- INR 3000 per person).
    *   Alternatively: Visit a local market like the Mir Alam Mandi to experience the local food culture.
*   Evening: Farewell Dinner: Enjoy a final Hyderabadi feast at a restaurant specializing in authentic cuisine. Consider **Jewel of Nizam** (more upscale) or a well-reviewed local restaurant. (Budget: INR 1000- INR 2000 for two).

**Day 7: Departure**

*   Morning: Enjoy a leisurely breakfast.
*   Check out of your hotel.
*   Depending on your flight time, you might have time for some last-minute souvenir shopping or a final visit to a place you enjoyed.
*   Transfer to Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD) for your flight back to Kolkata (CCU). (Taxi Cost: INR 500 - INR 800).

**IV. Important Tips & Considerations:**

*   **Bargaining:**  Bargaining is expected in local markets like Laad Bazaar and Koti.
*   **Dress Code:** Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
*   **Water:** Drink bottled water only.
*   **Health:** Consult your doctor about necessary vaccinations and medications.
*   **Safety:** Be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against petty theft.
*   **Language:** Hindi and Telugu are widely spoken. English is also common in tourist areas.
*   **Weather:** Check the weather forecast before you go and pack accordingly.
*   **Tourist Guides:** Consider hiring a local guide for a more in-depth experience of historical sites.  You can find guides through tourist information centers or online platforms.
*   **Transportation Apps:**  Download Ola and Uber for convenient transportation.
*   **Payment:**  While credit/debit cards are accepted in some establishments, it's advisable to carry cash for smaller vendors and transportation.  UPI payments are also widely accepted.
*   **Emergency Contacts:** Keep a list of important emergency contacts handy (police, ambulance, your embassy).
*    **Stay Updated:** COVID-19 guidelines and restrictions can change. Check the latest travel advisories before your trip.
