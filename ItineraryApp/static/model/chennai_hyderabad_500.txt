**II. Detailed Itinerary:**

**Day 1: Arrival and Historical Hyderabad**

*   **Morning (7:00 AM - 12:00 PM):**
    *   **7:00 AM:** Depart from Chennai (MAA).  (Adjust this based on your booked flight.)
    *   **8:30 AM - 9:30 AM:** Arrive at Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD), Hyderabad.
    *   **9:30 AM - 10:30 AM:**  Take an airport bus or pre-booked taxi/Uber to your hotel. (Approx. $5-$10 for taxi/Uber, less for bus).
    *   **10:30 AM - 11:30 AM:** Check into your hotel, freshen up.
    *   **11:30 AM - 12:00 PM:**  Travel to Charminar area (use the Metro or an auto-rickshaw – negotiate the fare in advance.  Expect to pay around $2-$4 for an auto-rickshaw depending on distance.)

*   **Afternoon (12:00 PM - 5:00 PM):**
    *   **12:00 PM - 1:00 PM:** Lunch near Charminar. Try Paradise Biryani (a famous Hyderabad restaurant, but can be pricier) or look for smaller local eateries for more affordable options. Budget $5-$8.
    *   **1:00 PM - 3:00 PM:** Explore Charminar.  Climb to the top for panoramic views (small entrance fee).  Bargain for souvenirs in the surrounding Laad Bazaar (bangles, jewelry, textiles).
    *   **3:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** Visit Mecca Masjid, a historic mosque near Charminar.  (Dress respectfully – cover shoulders and knees.)

*   **Evening (5:00 PM - 10:00 PM):**
    *   **5:00 PM - 6:00 PM:**  Have chai and Osmania biscuits at a local tea stall. (Very cheap - $1-$2).
    *   **6:00 PM - 8:00 PM:**  Visit Chowmahalla Palace, the former seat of the Nizams. Explore the palace grounds and learn about the history of Hyderabad.
    *   **8:00 PM - 9:00 PM:** Dinner at a local restaurant in the Old City. Try Hyderabadi Haleem (seasonal – typically available during Ramadan) or other local dishes. Budget $5-$10.
    *   **9:00 PM - 10:00 PM:**  Return to your hotel.

**Day 2: Golconda Fort and Hussain Sagar Lake**

*   **Morning (8:00 AM - 12:00 PM):**
    *   **8:00 AM - 9:00 AM:** Breakfast at your hotel or a nearby restaurant.
    *   **9:00 AM - 10:00 AM:**  Travel to Golconda Fort (take a bus, taxi, or auto-rickshaw).
    *   **10:00 AM - 12:00 PM:** Explore Golconda Fort.  Hire a local guide for a more enriching experience (negotiate the price beforehand). Explore the fort's architecture, listen to the acoustics, and learn about its history.

*   **Afternoon (12:00 PM - 5:00 PM):**
    *   **12:00 PM - 1:00 PM:** Lunch near Golconda Fort.
    *   **1:00 PM - 3:00 PM:** Visit the Qutb Shahi Tombs, a complex of tombs of the rulers of the Qutb Shahi dynasty.
    *   **3:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** Travel to Hussain Sagar Lake (use a bus or auto-rickshaw).

*   **Evening (5:00 PM - 10:00 PM):**
    *   **5:00 PM - 7:00 PM:**  Enjoy a boat ride on Hussain Sagar Lake.  Visit the giant Buddha statue in the middle of the lake.
    *   **7:00 PM - 8:00 PM:**  Stroll along Necklace Road, a scenic promenade around the lake.
    *   **8:00 PM - 9:00 PM:** Dinner at Eat Street by the Lake for a variety of food options and experience the night life, Budget ($10 - $15).
    *   **9:00 PM - 10:00 PM:** Return to your hotel.

**Day 3: Salar Jung Museum and Departure**

*   **Morning (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM):**
    *   **9:00 AM - 10:00 AM:** Breakfast at your hotel.
    *   **10:00 AM - 12:00 PM:** Visit the Salar Jung Museum, home to a vast collection of art, artifacts, and manuscripts.

*   **Afternoon (12:00 PM - 3:00 PM):**
    *   **12:00 PM - 1:00 PM:** Lunch near the museum.
    *   **1:00 PM - 2:00 PM:**  Do some last-minute souvenir shopping at a local market (avoid tourist traps).
    *   **2:00 PM - 3:00 PM:**  Travel to Rajiv Gandhi International Airport (HYD).

*   **Evening (3:00 PM onwards):**
    *   **3:00 PM - 5:00 PM:** Check in for your flight.
    *   **6:00 PM:** Depart from Hyderabad (HYD).  (Adjust based on your flight time).
    *   Arrive back in Chennai.

**IV. Important Considerations and Tips:**

*   **Bargaining:**  Bargain respectfully when shopping in markets and for auto-rickshaw fares.
*   **Safety:**  Be aware of your surroundings, especially in crowded areas. Keep your valuables secure.
*   **Hydration:** Drink plenty of bottled water to stay hydrated, especially during the warmer months.
*   **Health:** Consult your doctor about necessary vaccinations and health precautions before traveling to India.
*   **Dress Code:** Dress modestly when visiting religious sites.
*   **Local Sim Card:** Consider purchasing a local SIM card for data and calls.
*   **Cash:** Carry enough cash for smaller transactions, as some establishments may not accept credit cards.
*   **Language:** While English is widely spoken in Hyderabad, learning a few basic Hindi or Telugu phrases can be helpful.
*   **Tourist Guides:** If you really want more information, ask for a government-approved tourist guide
*   **Food Safety:** Be mindful of where you eat. Opt for reputable restaurants and avoid street food that looks questionable.
