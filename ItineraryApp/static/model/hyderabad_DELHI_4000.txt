Okay, here's a detailed trip plan from Hyderabad to Delhi for a mix of historical sightseeing, cultural experiences, delicious food, and using travel resources, all within a $4000 budget.  This assumes travel for one person.  Adjust for multiple people.  Prices are estimates and fluctuate.

**Assumptions:**

*   **Trip Duration:** 7 Days / 6 Nights (adjust as needed)
*   **Currency:** USD (all prices in USD)
*   **Travel Style:** Comfortable but not ultra-luxury.  Prioritizes good value and immersive experiences.
*   **Flights:** Economy class.
*   **Hotels:** 3-4 star range, well-reviewed, and centrally located.
*   **Food:** Mix of restaurants, street food, and some nicer dining experiences.
*   **Intended Audience:** Solo traveler

**Pre-Trip Planning (Crucial)**

1.  **Flights Booking (ASAP):**
    *   **Websites:** Use Skyscanner, Google Flights, Kayak, Expedia, or directly book with airlines.
    *   **Tip:** Book at least 2-3 months in advance for the best deals.  Consider flying mid-week (Tuesday/Wednesday) for lower fares.
    *   **Estimated Round-Trip Flight Cost:** $300 - $500 (economy).  Book this IMMEDIATELY to secure your budget.  You can set up price alerts on these websites to track fare changes.

2.  **Visa (If Applicable):** Ensure your visa (if needed) is valid for India.  Apply well in advance.

3.  **Travel Insurance:**  Essential for medical emergencies, cancellations, and lost luggage.  Compare policies on websites like World Nomads, Allianz, or Travel Guard.  Estimated cost: $50 - $100.

4.  **Hotel Research & Booking:**
    *   **Platforms:** Booking.com, Agoda, Expedia, Hotels.com, MakeMyTrip (Indian site)
    *   **Location:** Central Delhi (Connaught Place, Karol Bagh, Paharganj areas are good).  Consider proximity to metro stations for easy transportation.
    *   **Read Reviews:**  Check TripAdvisor, Google Reviews, and the hotel booking platform reviews.
    *   **Book Flexible Rates:**  Opt for cancellation policies that allow you to change your plans if needed.

5.  **Tourist Guides:**
    *   **Websites:** Viator, GetYourGuide, TripAdvisor Experiences.
    *   **Options:**
        *   **Private Guide:** More expensive but offers personalized attention.
        *   **Group Tours:** More budget-friendly and a great way to meet other travelers.
    *   **Consider:** Historical walking tours, food tours, and day trips to nearby cities.

6.  **Transportation Schedules:**
    *   **Delhi Metro:**  Download the DMRC (Delhi Metro Rail Corporation) app for route maps and schedules.
    *   **Ola/Uber:**  These ride-hailing apps are readily available in Delhi.
    *   **Local Buses:**  More budget-friendly but can be crowded.  Use Google Maps for bus routes.
    *   **Auto-rickshaws:** Negotiate the fare *before* you get in.

7.  **Weather Forecasts:** Check AccuWeather, The Weather Channel, or Google Weather a few days before your trip to pack appropriately. Delhi can have extreme temperatures (hot summers, cold winters).

8.  **Indian SIM Card:**  Buy a local SIM card at the airport upon arrival for easy internet access and local calls.  Airtel or Jio are good options.

**Detailed Itinerary**

**Day 1: Arrival in Delhi & Old Delhi Immersion**

*   **Morning:**
    *   Arrive at Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL).
    *   Purchase a local SIM card.
    *   Take a pre-paid taxi or Ola/Uber to your hotel in Delhi. (Estimated cost: $15-$25)

*   **Hotel:** *The Park New Delhi* (Connaught Place) -  Stylish hotel with good reviews. (Estimated cost: $150/night, total for 6 nights = $900) Alternatively, consider *Hotel Metropolitan*, or *Radisson Blu Marina Connaught Place*
*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Lunch:** Karim's (Old Delhi) - Famous for its Mughlai cuisine, especially kebabs and biryani. (Estimated cost: $15-20)
    *   **Old Delhi Exploration:**
        *   Visit Jama Masjid (India's largest mosque).
        *   Explore Chandni Chowk market (spices, textiles, street food).
        *   Take a rickshaw ride through the narrow lanes. (Negotiate price beforehand - around $5-$10)

*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner:**  Parathe Wali Gali (Chandni Chowk) - A street dedicated to various stuffed parathas (Indian flatbreads).  (Estimated cost: $5-10)
    *   Return to your hotel.

**Day 2:  Historical Delhi - Mughal Grandeur**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Breakfast:** At your hotel or a local cafe. (Estimated cost: $5-10)
    *   **Humayun's Tomb:** A magnificent Mughal-era tomb, precursor to the Taj Mahal. (Entry fee: ~$8)

*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Lunch:**  Khan Market - Lots of restaurants and cafes offering various cuisines. (Estimated cost: $15-25)
    *   **Qutub Minar:**  A UNESCO World Heritage Site, the tallest minaret in India. (Entry fee: ~$8)

*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner:** Indian Accent (The Lodhi Hotel) - High-end dining experience featuring modern Indian cuisine. (Estimated cost: $75-100) **Consider this as your splurge meal.**
    *   Alternatively, try a restaurant in Hauz Khas Village for a more casual vibe.

**Day 3:  New Delhi - Colonial Architecture & Gardens**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Breakfast:** At your hotel or a local cafe.
    *   **India Gate:** A war memorial. Stroll around the gardens.
    *   **Rashtrapati Bhavan:** The President's House (view from the outside).  Check for tour availability online in advance.

*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Lunch:**  Saravana Bhavan (Connaught Place) - South Indian vegetarian cuisine. (Estimated cost: $10-15)
    *   **Lodhi Garden:**  Historical garden with tombs and monuments.

*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner:**  United Coffee House (Connaught Place) - A historic coffee house serving Indian and Continental dishes. (Estimated cost: $20-30)
    *   Consider a Bollywood dance show (check availability online - Ticket cost: $20-$40)

**Day 4:  Cultural Experiences & Crafts**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Breakfast:** At your hotel or a local cafe.
    *   **National Museum:** Explore Indian art, history, and culture. (Entry fee: ~$8)

*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Lunch:** Dilli Haat - A craft village with food stalls from various Indian states. (Entry fee: ~$1)  Sample regional cuisines. (Estimated food cost: $15-20)
    *   **Shopping:**  Janpath Market or Khan Market for souvenirs and handicrafts.

*   **Evening:**
    *   **Dinner:**  Bukhara (ITC Maurya Hotel) - Famous for its North-West Frontier cuisine, especially the Dal Bukhara. (Estimated cost: $60-80) Another splurge option.
    *   Consider a Sufi music performance at Nizamuddin Dargah (check timings beforehand – usually Thursday evenings).

**Day 5: Day Trip to Agra (Taj Mahal)**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Early Start:** Take the Gatimaan Express train to Agra (departs from New Delhi Railway Station). Book tickets in advance on the IRCTC website (www.irctc.co.in). (Estimated train fare: $20-40 round trip).
    *   **Alternatively:** Hire a private car and driver for the day (more expensive - around $100-$150).

*   **Agra:**
    *   **Taj Mahal:**  Visit the Taj Mahal (Entry fee: ~$15). Hire a local guide for a richer experience (negotiate the price - $20-$30).
    *   **Agra Fort:**  Explore the Agra Fort, another UNESCO World Heritage Site. (Entry fee: ~$8)

*   **Lunch:**  Pinch of Spice (Agra) - Restaurant serving North Indian and Mughlai cuisine. (Estimated cost: $20-30)
*   **Evening:**
    *   Return to Delhi by train or car.
    *   **Dinner:**  Street food near your hotel or a restaurant of your choice. (Estimated cost: $10-20)

**Day 6:  Temples and Relaxation**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Breakfast:** At your hotel or a local cafe.
    *   **Akshardham Temple:** A modern Hindu temple complex with intricate carvings and exhibitions. (Entry is free, but exhibitions have fees).
    *   **Lotus Temple:** A Baháʼí House of Worship known for its lotus-like shape.

*   **Afternoon:**
    *   **Lunch:**  Local restaurant near the temples. (Estimated cost: $10-15)
    *   **Relaxation:** Get an Ayurvedic massage at a reputable spa. (Estimated cost: $40-60)

*   **Evening:**
    *   **Farewell Dinner:**  Choose your favorite cuisine and restaurant from the trip.  Reflect on your experiences.
    *   Last-minute souvenir shopping.

**Day 7: Departure**

*   **Morning:**
    *   **Breakfast:** At your hotel.
    *   Check out of your hotel.
    *   Transfer to Indira Gandhi International Airport (DEL) for your flight home.

**Important Notes:**

*   **Bargaining:** Bargain when shopping in markets and for auto-rickshaw fares.
*   **Dress Code:** Dress modestly when visiting religious sites (cover shoulders and knees).
*   **Safety:** Be aware of your surroundings and take precautions against petty theft.
*   **Health:** Drink bottled water and be cautious about street food (choose places that look clean and busy).
*   **Tipping:** Tipping is customary in India (10-15% in restaurants).
*   **Flexibility:** This is just a suggested itinerary. Feel free to adjust it to your interests and preferences.  Don't be afraid to deviate from the plan!

**Tips for Saving Money:**

*   **Travel during the off-season (summer months are very hot).**
*   **Stay in budget-friendly hotels or guesthouses.**
*   **Eat at local restaurants and street food stalls (be mindful of hygiene).**
*   **Use the Delhi Metro for transportation.**
*   **Take advantage of free activities like walking tours and visiting parks.**
*   **Negotiate prices when shopping and for transportation.**

By following this detailed itinerary and the tips provided, you can have an amazing and memorable trip to Delhi within your $4000 budget! Remember to book your flights and hotels in advance to secure the best deals. Enjoy your trip!
